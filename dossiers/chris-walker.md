# Chris Walker

**Lens:** Most B2B revenue is created by demand you never captured credit
for — the job is to create real market demand first, and only then build
the lightest-touch capture mechanism, instead of optimizing capture metrics
that were never the source of the revenue.

## Core frameworks

- **Demand creation vs. demand capture** (Refine Labs content, LinkedIn
  corpus, podcast "Inside the ICP" / earlier "Sweat the Startup"): demand
  creation builds market awareness and preference before a buyer is
  actively searching; demand capture (SEO, paid search, forms) only
  intercepts demand that creation already produced. Most B2B marketing
  budget is misallocated toward capture because capture is easier to
  attribute.
- **The dark funnel** (Refine Labs / LinkedIn corpus): the large share of
  buyer research and influence — communities, word of mouth, podcasts,
  peer conversations, dark social shares — that standard attribution tools
  cannot see or credit, meaning last-touch and multi-touch attribution both
  systematically undercount what actually moved the buyer.
- **Self-reported attribution** (Refine Labs methodology): asking buyers
  directly, at the point of conversion, how they first heard of the
  company — used as a corrective data source specifically because
  platform-based attribution can't see the dark funnel.
- **The B2B revenue "system of understanding"** (LinkedIn corpus, Refine
  Labs pods content): a model connecting brand/demand creation activity to
  pipeline and revenue outcomes over longer time horizons than
  campaign-level attribution windows allow.

## Documented positions

- Cost-per-lead and MQL-volume as primary marketing KPIs actively reward
  the wrong behavior — they push teams toward gated content and form-fills
  that generate low-intent leads while starving the demand-creation work
  that produces real pipeline. — *LinkedIn corpus / Refine Labs content*
- Marketing attribution software (first-touch, multi-touch, last-touch
  models) is fundamentally broken for B2B because it can't measure the dark
  funnel where most buying decisions are actually influenced — teams that
  trust these dashboards are optimizing against noise. (Contrarian: directly
  attacks the martech-attribution-stack orthodoxy much of B2B marketing ops
  is built on.) — *LinkedIn corpus*
- Gated content (forms in front of ebooks/whitepapers) trades a small
  amount of contact data for a large amount of lost reach and goodwill;
  most gated content should be ungated. — *LinkedIn corpus / podcast*
- The SDR-outbound-plus-MQL-quota model that dominates B2B SaaS go-to-market
  is a local optimization that looks efficient on a spreadsheet and
  destroys long-term brand and demand creation. — *podcast / LinkedIn
  corpus*
- Founder- and executive-led organic content (LinkedIn, podcasts) now
  outperforms traditional brand advertising for B2B demand creation because
  it's the medium buyers actually spend attention in. — *LinkedIn corpus*

## Signature questions

- Is this activity creating demand, or just capturing demand we already
  created somewhere else?
- What would self-reported attribution tell us here that the dashboard
  can't?
- How much of this "pipeline" is actually just intercepted branded search
  from demand we get no attribution credit for?
- If we ungated this, would it still be worth making?

## Best for / blind spots

**Best for:** B2B SaaS go-to-market critique, attribution-model sanity
checks, diagnosing MQL/SQL pipelines that look efficient but underperform
on revenue, brand-vs-performance budget arguments.
**Blind spots:** Corpus lives in LinkedIn posts, podcast appearances, and
Refine Labs practitioner content rather than a peer-reviewed or long-form
published book, so claims are harder to independently audit; framework is
built from a narrow set of high-growth VC-backed B2B SaaS companies and
translates less directly to regulated, motion-constrained, or long-cycle
enterprise categories with compliance-driven demand.
