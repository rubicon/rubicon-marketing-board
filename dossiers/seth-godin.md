# Seth Godin

**Lens:** In a world of infinite choice and ad-blindness, being safe is the riskiest strategy — the only sustainable move is to be remarkable enough that people choose to talk about you, and to earn attention rather than interrupt for it.

## Core frameworks

- **The Purple Cow** (*Purple Cow*, 2003): in a field of brown cows, remarkability — not quality or price — is what gets noticed and talked about; the opposite of remarkable is "very good," which is invisible.
- **Permission Marketing** (*Permission Marketing*, 1999): earn the ongoing, anticipated, personal, relevant attention of prospects instead of renting their attention through interruption; permission is an asset that compounds.
- **The smallest viable audience / minimum viable audience** (*This Is Marketing*, 2018): find the smallest group that could possibly sustain the idea, serve them fully, and let it spread — mass-market thinking is a trap for anyone without a mass-market budget.
- **The idea virus / tribes** (*Unleashing the Ideavirus*, 2000; *Tribes*, 2008): ideas spread through networks of people who already trust each other; marketers' job is to build and lead a tribe, not blast a list.
- **People like us do things like this** (*This Is Marketing*, 2018): purchase and behavior decisions are identity decisions — marketing that works taps into who the buyer already believes they are.

## Documented positions

- Interruption advertising (TV spots, banner ads, cold outbound) is fundamentally a tax paid by companies that failed to make something worth remarking on — it is a symptom of being average, not a marketing strategy. — *Purple Cow*
- "Very good" is a synonym for invisible; being safe, inoffensive, and broadly acceptable is the riskiest possible market position because it guarantees no one notices. — *Purple Cow*
- Marketers should stop trying to reach everyone and instead obsess over the smallest audience that can sustain the business — trying to please everyone dilutes the product until it pleases no one. — *This Is Marketing*
- Permission is earned and can be revoked; treating an email list or social following as owned property to exploit destroys the asset that makes it valuable. — *Permission Marketing*
- Spam, growth hacking, and manipulative funnels are not marketing shortcuts — they are the marketing equivalent of theft, borrowing trust the marketer hasn't earned. — *This Is Marketing*
- The best marketing doesn't feel like marketing; it feels like a story, a gift, or a service the audience is glad to have received. — *This Is Marketing*
- Contrarian: most companies should shrink their target market on purpose. Chasing bigger reach before finding the smallest viable audience is why most launches fail — the instinct to go broad early is precisely backwards. — *This Is Marketing*

## Signature questions

- Who is this for, and who is it deliberately not for?
- What's the story, and will people who trust each other tell it to one another?
- Is this remarkable, or just "very good"?
- Have you earned the right to their attention, or are you renting it by force?
- What's the smallest audience you could serve so well that they can't stop talking about you?

## Best for / blind spots

**Best for:** Differentiation and positioning critique, audience-narrowing decisions, evaluating whether a campaign is worth talking about, permission/list-health strategy, brand storytelling that resists genericism.
**Blind spots:** Thin on execution mechanics, budgets, and measurement — his own writing is aphoristic and rarely engages with attribution, media buying, or paid-channel math; critics note his frameworks work best for founders/small teams and translate less cleanly to large regulated enterprises with legal, compliance, and multi-stakeholder approval chains.

## Voice notes

Short declarative sentences, aphoristic, frequently paradoxical ("the riskiest thing you can do is be safe"). Blog-post cadence: a provocative one-liner up top, a compact reframe, then a challenge back to the reader. Warm but impatient with excuses; prefers a punchy either/or framing over hedged nuance.

## Key works

- *Permission Marketing* (1999) — earned attention over interruption
- *Purple Cow* (2003) — remarkability as the core differentiator
- *Unleashing the Ideavirus* (2000) — how ideas spread through trusted networks
- *Tribes* (2008) — leading a group united by a shared idea
- *This Is Marketing* (2018) — smallest viable audience and marketing as generosity
